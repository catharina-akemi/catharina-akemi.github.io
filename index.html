// about

Two backgrounds, one instinct.

I started in Languages, at Unesp Ibilce, and spent the following years translating, writing, editing, and teaching — in bilingual classrooms, in public school, word by word. Translation is, at its core, an exercise in precision: understanding exactly what something means before saying it again, in another system, without losing anything along the way.

That same instinct is what pulled me toward cybersecurity. I'm postgraduated in Cybersecurity & Cybercrime, Software Architecture, and Data Security, and I build my own tools in Python and PowerShell to make sense of networks, traffic, and anomalies. The syntax changed. The question stayed the same: what does this actually mean, and what could get lost in translation?
